date: 2026-05-17

I've been consuming things for a while now — papers, articles, videos, the occasional rabbit hole that starts with one question and ends three hours later somewhere completely unexpected. The consuming was never the problem. The forgetting was.

There's something quietly frustrating about reading something that genuinely changes how you think, underlining it in your head, and then watching it dissolve into the general fog of everything else you've half-absorbed. A few weeks pass and you remember that you read something about, say, attention mechanisms or scaling laws or whatever — but not what you actually thought about it. Not the part that mattered.

So this is the attempt at a fix.

This blog is essentially a reading log with pretensions. It'll have AI and ML papers I'm working through, articles that caught me off-guard, videos worth watching twice, and notes that might embarrass me later but feel useful now. It is not meant to be authoritative. It's meant to be honest — a record of what I understood, what confused me, and occasionally, what I found beautiful about an idea.

I don't know if this will be useful to anyone else. Maybe. But the real audience is future me, who will probably not remember reading any of this, and will at least have somewhere to look.

The ritual is simple: read, think, write a little, move on. No pressure to be comprehensive. No pretending I got everything. Just the act of pausing long enough to leave a mark somewhere.

And so on. Keep it honest, keep it curious.
